# Seafood Processing - General Inspection Checklist

## Overview

This checklist covers the standard inspection procedures for receiving and assessing seafood products. Based on industry best practices (Beaver Street Fisheries Quality Seafood Inspection Methodology, FDA guidelines).

## Inspection Workflow

```
Receiving → Package Assessment → Product Quality Check → Organoleptic Evaluation → Grading → Documentation
```

---

## 1. RECEIVING & TEMPERATURE CHECK

### Trailer/Container Assessment

- [ ] Check condition of trailer/container for cleanliness
- [ ] Document external temperature (target: frozen ≤ 0°F, refrigerated 32-38°F)
- [ ] Record multiple internal temperatures from trailer/container
- [ ] Note any evidence of temperature abuse (wet cardboard, frost, pliable product)
- [ ] Check condition of packaging materials (dampness, deterioration)
- [ ] Record date, time, and receiving conditions

### Critical Limits (Temperature)

| Product Type | Critical Limit | Acceptable Range |
|--------------|----------------|------------------|
| Frozen Seafood | ≤ 0°F | -18°C or below |
| Refrigerated Cooked/RTE | ≤ 40°F | 32-38°F (optimal) |
| Molluscan Shellfish (Frozen) | ≤ 10°F | 0-10°F |

---

## 2. PACKAGING ASSESSMENT

### Master Case/Outer Packaging

#### Acceptable Condition
- [ ] No visible damage to master cases
- [ ] Cases clean and free of dust
- [ ] Proper closure/sealing (no gaps)
- [ ] Labels intact and readable

#### Reasonable Condition (Minor Issues)
- [ ] Minor case damage (small tears without exposed product)
- [ ] Light to moderate dirt/dust accumulation
- [ ] Deformed cases (structurally sound)
- [ ] Minor cardboard degradation

#### Unacceptable Condition
- [ ] Heavy dust/filth accumulation
- [ ] Severe damage/busted sealing
- [ ] Exposed product
- [ ] Cases falling apart or very weak

### Inner Packaging/Product Protection

- [ ] Poly bags/vacuum packs intact (no punctures)
- [ ] Proper sealing (vacuum packs sealed completely)
- [ ] Poly bags folded over product to prevent dehydration
- [ ] Protective padding present (if required)
- [ ] No signs of freezer burn or excessive frost

---

## 3. LABELING COMPLIANCE CHECK

### Master Case Labels (Domestic)

- [ ] Country of Origin
- [ ] Method of Production (Wild caught, Farmed, etc.)
- [ ] Packer and/or Distributor name
- [ ] Net Weight with units (e.g., "40 lbs, 4×10 lbs")
- [ ] Date Codes (Production date, Best By, Sell By)

### Master Case Labels (Import & Private Label)

Add to domestic requirements:
- [ ] Shipment Number
- [ ] Item Number (master/inner)
- [ ] UPC (master/inner) - must scan correctly
- [ ] Nutritional Information
- [ ] Ingredients list
- [ ] Cooking Instructions
- [ ] Allergen Statement

### Inner Pack/Retail Labeling

- [ ] Product Description (clear product identification)
- [ ] Item/Lot numbers match master case
- [ ] All labels securely attached
- [ ] Printing quality (legible, not smudged)
- [ ] Date codes match/are consistent

### COOL Labeling (Country of Origin)

- [ ] Country of origin clearly stated
- [ ] Method of production indicated
- [ ] No missing or obscured origin information

---

## 4. PHYSICAL PRODUCT INSPECTION

### Receiving Quantities

- [ ] Count master cases received
- [ ] Count inner units/samples as needed
- [ ] Record pallet configuration
- [ ] Verify counts match shipping documents

### Temperature Abuse Indicators

Watch for:
- [ ] Wet or refrozen cardboard
- [ ] Master/inner cases stuck together
- [ ] Excessive frost on product
- [ ] Loss of glaze on frozen products
- [ ] Pliable/thawed product (frozen items)
- [ ] IQF clumping (product stuck together with ice)
- [ ] Objectionable odors
- [ ] Discolored glaze (refrozen staining)

### Dehydration Check

- [ ] Open sample units to assess internal condition
- [ ] Check for product shrinkage
- [ ] Look for icy deposits or white spots
- [ ] Assess glaze coverage uniformity
- [ ] **Zero tolerance** for significant dehydration

---

## 5. ORGANOLEPTIC (SENSORY) EVALUATION

### Odor Assessment

#### Good
- [ ] Fresh ocean air, clean seaweed aroma
- [ ] No decomposition odors
- [ ] No aged or freezer burn smell

#### Reasonably Good
- [ ] Slight fishy smell (acceptable for species)
- [ ] Neutral to characteristic odor
- [ ] No strong off-odors

#### Fair
- [ ] Slight to moderate fishy/cardboardy odor
- [ ] Minor aged/freezer odors acceptable
- [ ] Not suitable for premium products

#### Poor / Not For Human Consumption
- [ ] Sour, ammonia, or fecal odors
- [ ] Strong putrid smell
- [ ] Rancid, cheesy, or moldy notes
- [ ] Any indication of decomposition

### Appearance & Color

#### Good
- [ ] Translucent and shiny (raw)
- [ ] Firm, resilient texture
- [ ] Moist appearance
- [ ] Proper species-specific coloring
- [ ] Sharp, clearly defined shape

#### Reasonably Good
- [ ] Slightly opaque (acceptable aging)
- [ ] Slightly firm texture
- [ ] Color slightly faded but acceptable
- [ ] Minor texture variations

#### Fair
- [ ] Moderately opaque
- [ ] Surface-dry appearance
- [ ] Faded color
- [ ] Minor texture inconsistency
- [ ] Acceptable for processed products

#### Poor / Not For Human Consumption
- [ ] Strongly opaque
- [ ] Dull, sticky, or grainy
- [ ] Yellowing or browning
- [ ] Mushy or mushy texture
- [ ] Loss of shape integrity

### Flavor/Taste (if applicable)

- [ ] Characteristic of fresh or frozen product
- [ ] No sour, sickly sweet, or ammonia notes
- [ ] No stale or chemical taste
- [ ] Product-specific flavor profile intact

### Texture

- [ ] Firm and moist (acceptable for product type)
- [ ] No mushiness or decomposition
- [ ] No excessive dryness (unless expected)
- [ ] Characteristic firmness for species

---

## 6. DEFECT DOCUMENTATION

### Record Any Defects Found

#### Product Defects
- [ ] Bones/cartilage in boneless fillets
- [ ] Parasites or worms present
- [ ] Bruises or discoloration
- [ ] Meat separation or texture breakdown
- [ ] Foreign material contamination
- [ ] Excessive breakage or damage

#### Workmanship Defects
- [ ] Poor trimming (uneven cuts)
- [ ] Excess skin on skinless products
- [ ] Improper sizing/portion weights
- [ ] Inconsistent thickness
- [ ] Improper fillet cuts

#### Species-Specific Defects
- **Crab**: Black/blue meat, excess barnacles, poor cleaning
- **Lobster**: Black bellies, soft shell, throat meat extension
- **Shrimp**: Long throat meat, sand veins, black spots
- **Scallops**: Discoloration (purple/gray), shell/grit, yellowing
- **Fin Fish**: Green bellies, excess parasites, bone retention

### Photography Documentation

If defects found:
- [ ] Photograph packaging (all sides with visible labels)
- [ ] Photograph damaged/defective product
- [ ] Include scale reference where applicable
- [ ] Document lighting and image quality
- [ ] Save with PO#, Lot#, and date codes

---

## 7. SAMPLING & GRADING

### Sampling Plan (by lot size)

| Lot Size | # Samples |
|----------|-----------|
| 3-200 cases | 3 samples |
| 201-500 cases | 5 samples |
| 501-1000 cases | 7 samples |
| 1001+ cases | 9 samples |

### Sampling Strategy

- [ ] Pull samples from multiple locations within load
- [ ] Sample from top, middle, and bottom of pallets
- [ ] Include multiple date codes if present
- [ ] Randomize selection (no cherry-picking)
- [ ] Document which cases sampled

### Weight Assessment (Frozen Products)

1. [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging and glaze)
2. [ ] Record glaze-on weight (with glaze)
3. [ ] Drain sample 2 minutes at 30° angle in No. 8 sieve
4. [ ] Record glaze-off weight
5. [ ] Calculate net weight and glaze percentage

**Glaze Calculation**:
```
% Glaze = (Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100 / Glaze On Wt
```

### Grading by Quality Level

- [ ] Assign overall product grade (Good, Reasonably Good, Fair, Poor)
- [ ] Record appearance rating
- [ ] Record odor/flavor rating
- [ ] Note any defects affecting grade
- [ ] Document count/size if applicable

---

## 8. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

### Accept Product If:

- [ ] Temperature maintained within critical limits
- [ ] Packaging is intact and properly labeled
- [ ] Sensory evaluation shows acceptable quality
- [ ] Defects (if any) are minor and documented
- [ ] Weight/count specifications met
- [ ] Odor/flavor indicates proper freshness

### Reject/Hold Product If:

- [ ] Temperature abuse evident or documented
- [ ] Odor indicates spoilage/decomposition
- [ ] Significant packaging damage or labeling issues
- [ ] Excessive dehydration present
- [ ] Foreign material or pest contamination
- [ ] Weight/count significantly below specification
- [ ] COOL labeling non-compliant
- [ ] Shellfish from non-certified supplier
- [ ] Appearance indicates safety risk

### Conditional Hold/Quarantine If:

- [ ] Minor defects require supervisor review
- [ ] Temperature slightly elevated (review exposure history)
- [ ] Documentation unclear or incomplete
- [ ] Awaiting third-party verification (species DNA, etc.)
- [ ] Pending supplier clarification

---

## 9. INSPECTION RECORD COMPLETION

### Information to Record

- [ ] Date and time of inspection
- [ ] Inspector name and warehouse location
- [ ] Supplier/vendor information
- [ ] Product description and item numbers
- [ ] Lot/batch numbers and date codes
- [ ] PO number and shipment reference
- [ ] Temperature readings (trailer, product)
- [ ] Sampling results (grades, weights, counts)
- [ ] Any defects found and documented
- [ ] Overall acceptance decision
- [ ] Photographs (file references)
- [ ] Inspector signature

### Status Codes

| Code | Status | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| O | OPEN | Product approved, available for sale |
| H | HACCP HOLD | Quality/safety concern, awaiting review |
| R | RETURN | Non-conforming, return to supplier |
| Q | QC HOLD | Inspection incomplete, pending results |
| M | COOL HOLD | Missing/incorrect origin labeling |
| I | INVENTORY HOLD | Reserved for special purpose |

---

## 10. TRACEABILITY & DOCUMENTATION

### Required Records (FDA HACCP)

- [ ] Receiving documentation with supplier info
- [ ] Temperature/time records
- [ ] QC inspection results and grades
- [ ] Lot/batch traceability information
- [ ] Any corrective actions taken
- [ ] Photographs of defects
- [ ] Approval/rejection decision
- [ ] Inspector identification

### Retention

- [ ] Keep records minimum 2 years from date of inspection
- [ ] Maintain digital and/or physical copies
- [ ] Link to product distribution records
- [ ] Enable rapid traceability if recall needed

## Special Procedures by Product Type

### Molluscan Shellfish

- [ ] Verify licensed processor on Interstate Certified Shellfish Shippers List (ICSSL)
- [ ] Check harvest area labeling
- [ ] Verify dealer certification number
- [ ] Record harvest date
- [ ] Confirm temperature monitoring (TTI/TTR if applicable)
- [ ] Document tag information for traceability

### Frozen Products

- [ ] Always check for dehydration/freezer burn
- [ ] Assess glaze condition and coverage
- [ ] Check for IQF clumping
- [ ] Verify proper thawing technique if testing
- [ ] Document moisture content if required

### Breaded/Coated Products

- [ ] Document breading percentage (should not exceed 52%)
- [ ] Assess coating adhesion and coverage
- [ ] Check for clumping or matting
- [ ] Verify breading color and quality

## Quick Reference: Decision Tree

```
ACCEPT?
├─ Temperature OK? NO → REJECT (Temperature Abuse)
├─ Packaging Intact? NO → REJECT/HOLD (Quality Issue)
├─ Labels Complete? NO → HOLD/COOL HOLD (Compliance)
├─ Odor OK? NO → REJECT (Spoilage Indicator)
├─ Appearance OK? NO → HOLD or REJECT
├─ Dehydration? NO → ACCEPT
├─ Defects Major? NO → ACCEPT (minor OK)
└─ All Specs Met? YES → ACCEPT
```
