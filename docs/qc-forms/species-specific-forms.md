# Species-Specific QC Inspection Forms

Detailed inspection procedures for common seafood processing products.

---

## CRAB PRODUCTS

### Inspection Methodology for Crab Legs (Snow, King, Blue Swimming)

#### Physical Inspection

**Weight Assessment**
- [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging and glaze)
- [ ] Select 6 full clusters or 10% of declared net weight
- [ ] Pull from top, middle, and bottom for uniform sample
- [ ] Record weight with glaze on (Glaze On Wt)
- [ ] Drain in No. 8 sieve for 2 minutes at 30° angle (70-80°F water)
- [ ] Record weight after draining (Glaze Off Wt)

**Calculations**
```
Calculated Net Wt = (Sub Sample Glaze Off Wt × Net Wt) / Sub Sample Glaze On Wt
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Count per Ten Pounds = (# Whole Usable Legs × 10) / (Net Wt Glaze Off - Wt Unusable Pieces)
```

**Size Grading**
- [ ] Weigh individual clusters in ounces
- [ ] Record sizes in grading section
- [ ] Document useable vs. unusable broken pieces

#### Appearance Inspection

- [ ] **Workmanship**: Properly cleaned and processed
  - Shoulder sections reasonably free of: carapace, mandibles, viscera, gills
  - No excessive meat trimming

- [ ] **Marine Growth**: Reasonably free from
  - Sea lice, moss, seaweed, barnacles
  - Should be minimal surface growth

- [ ] **Scarring**: Reasonable freedom from
  - Excess scratches or scar marks
  - Light scarring acceptable

- [ ] **Mottling** (Tanning):
  - Light to moderate tanning: ACCEPTABLE
  - Moderate to heavy tanning: UNACCEPTABLE

#### Organoleptic (Sensory) Inspection

- [ ] **Odor/Flavor**: Indicative of freshly caught/processed crab
  - Should smell: Fresh, sweet, ocean-like
  - Should NOT smell: Sour, sickly sweet, ammonia

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist
  - Should NOT be: Soft or mushy (indicates decomposition)

- [ ] **Color**: Indicative of live, freshly processed crab
  - Should NOT have: Black or blue meat (decomposition indicator)

#### Broken Crab Classification

- **Full Cluster**: 3 walking legs + claw arm attached (trailer leg optional)
- **Useable Broken**: Loose whole major appendages ± shoulder
- **Unusable Broken**: Shoulder sections, broken legs, tips, pieces

### Pasteurized Crabmeat Inspection (Canned)

#### Receiving

**Temperature Critical Limits**
- Acceptable range: 32.5°F - 38°F
- Critical limit: ≤ 40°F
- All shipments must include Time/Temperature Indicator (TTI) or Recorder (TTR)
  - Exception: Trips < 4 hours from origin

#### Physical Inspection

- [ ] Place unopened can on scale → Record as Gross Weight
- [ ] Open can and empty meat + juices onto scale → Record as Net Weight
- [ ] Smell meat for off-odors (sour, sickly sweet, ammonia)
- [ ] Sift through meat and record any:
  - Shell fragments
  - Cartilage pieces
  - Foreign material
- [ ] Assess texture: Should be firm, moist, white
- [ ] Assess color: Species and grade appropriate

#### Crabmeat Grades (Hand-Picked)

| Grade | Description | Appearance |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Colossal/Jumbo Lump | Largest unbroken muscles (swimming legs) | Large white pieces |
| Super Lump | Smaller/broken pieces of jumbo lump | Large white flakes |
| Lump | Remaining body meat with some flake | Mix of white meat |
| Special | Fine texture meat from body | Consistent texture |
| Claw | Brown meat from claws and legs | Brown color |
| Claw Fingers | First section of claw (shell partially removed) | Partial shell |

## LOBSTER PRODUCTS

### Inspection Methodology for Lobster Tails

#### Sampling & Defect Check

- [ ] Pull samples from random locations, multiple pallets
- [ ] Sample different production date codes
- [ ] Check for temperature abuse indicators:
  - Wet cardboard, frost, pliable product
  - Clumping (product stuck together)
  - Objectionable odors

#### Physical Inspection

**Weight Assessment**
- [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging/wrap and glaze)
- [ ] Count number of lobster tails
- [ ] Calculate tare weight (multiply tail count × avg bag weight)
- [ ] Randomly select 6 tails or 10% of declared net weight
- [ ] Remove poly wrap and record weight with glaze (Glaze On Wt)
- [ ] Drain in No. 8 sieve, belly portion down, 2 minutes at 30°
- [ ] Record weight after draining (Glaze Off Wt)

**Size Specifications**
- [ ] Weigh each sampled tail in ounces
- [ ] Acceptable tolerance: ±½ ounce of declared size
- [ ] Exception: 10-12 oz range allows 9.5-12 oz

**Calculations**
```
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Calculated Net Wt = (Sub Sample Glaze Off Wt × Net Wt) / Sub Sample Glaze On Wt
```

#### Appearance Defects (Check Each)

- [ ] **Black Bellies**: Dark area on belly membrane/anal area
  - Minor: Acceptable
  - Noticeable: Document for grading

- [ ] **Soft Shell**: Shell soft to touch, may appear translucent
  - Minor: Acceptable for some products
  - Significant: May indicate molting issues

- [ ] **Excessive Throat Meat**: Extends >1.5" past shell portion
  - May appear dirty or gray
  - Indicates improper processing

- [ ] **Broken/Cracked Shell**: Partially broken or cracked shell
  - Document location and severity

- [ ] **Broken/Missing Tails**:
  - Missing or damaged tail fins
  - Middle fin called "telson"

#### Organoleptic Inspection

- [ ] **Odor**: Freshly caught and processed lobster
  - Fresh ocean aroma
  - NO sour, sickly sweet, or ammonia notes

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist
  - NO soft or mushy (decomposition indicator)

- [ ] **Color**: Indicative of freshly caught lobster
  - Light tan/white meat expected
  - NO gray or darkened meat (spoilage)

## SHRIMP PRODUCTS

### Inspection Methodology for Shrimp (IQF, Breaded, Block)

#### Sampling Plan

- [ ] Pull samples from multiple random locations
- [ ] Sample different pallets and date codes
- [ ] Check for temperature abuse:
  - IQF clumping (product frozen together)
  - Excessive frost, pliable texture
  - Objectionable odors

#### Weight & Glaze Assessment (IQF)

- [ ] Record gross weight (packaging + glaze + shrimp)
- [ ] Randomly select 1-2 lbs minimum subsample
- [ ] Record glaze-on weight

**De-glaze Procedure**
- [ ] Place shrimp in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Spray/rinse all visible glaze (70-80°F water)
- [ ] Drain for 2 minutes at 30° angle
- [ ] Record glaze-off weight

**Calculations**
```
Calculated Net Wt = (Sub Sample Glaze Off Wt × Net Wt) / Sub Sample Glaze On Wt
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Count per Pound = (# Whole Usable Pieces × 100) / (Glaze Off Wt - Unusable Wt)
```

#### Breaded Shrimp Inspection

- [ ] Place breaded shrimp in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Spray remove all breading (70-80°F water)
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30° angle
- [ ] Record shrimp weight (de-breaded)

**Breading Calculation**
```
% Breading = ((Bread On Wt - Bread Off Wt) × 100) / Bread On Wt
NOTE: Should NOT exceed 52%
```

#### Block Frozen Shrimp

- [ ] Place block in plastic bag
- [ ] Submerge under running water until thawed (70-80°F)
- [ ] Place thawed shrimp in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30° angle
- [ ] Record weight as Calculated Net Weight

#### Shrimp Sizing Standards

Common commercial counts (headless, shell-on):
- U/10 (under 10 per lb) = Jumbo
- 16/20 = Large
- 21/25 = Medium-Large
- 31/35 = Medium
- 36/40 = Medium-Small
- 56/60 = Small
- 61/70 = Extra Small

#### Appearance Defects (Document Any)

- [ ] Long throat meat
- [ ] Meat discoloration (brown, gray)
- [ ] Sand veins (visible tract)
- [ ] Black spots on meat/tail
- [ ] Brown meat (indicates improper handling)
- [ ] Tail rot or black tail
- [ ] Soft tail
- [ ] Chip tail
- [ ] Miscuts
- [ ] Button hole (failed deveining)
- [ ] IQF clumping
- [ ] Breaded shrimp clumping

#### Organoleptic Assessment

**Odor/Flavor** - Should smell fresh, characteristic
- NO: Moderate-strong geosmin (muddy/grassy)
- NO: Sour or ammonia
- NO: Fecal or putrid
- NO: Stale cardboardy
- NO: Petroleum/diesel/chemical notes

**Texture** - Firm and moist
- NO: Soft or mushy (decomposition)
- NO: Rubbery texture (phosphate abuse indicator)

**Color** - Should be appropriate for species
- NO: Black spots on meat/tail (decomposition)
- NO: Reddish blotches (decomposition)
- NO: Translucency (phosphate abuse indicator)

## SCALLOP PRODUCTS

### Inspection Methodology for Scallops (IQF, Breaded, Block)

#### Weight Assessment (IQF)

- [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging and glaze)
- [ ] Randomly select 1-2 lbs minimum subsample
- [ ] Record weight with glaze on

**De-glaze**
- [ ] Place in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Drain for 2 minutes at 30° angle (70-80°F water)
- [ ] Record glaze-off weight

**Calculations**
```
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Count per Pound = (# Whole Usable Pieces × 100) / (Glaze Off Wt - Unusable Wt)
```

#### Breaded Scallops

- [ ] Place evenly in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Spray remove all breading
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30°
- [ ] Record weight

#### Sizing Standards

| Size | Count per lb |
|------|-------------|
| U/10 | Under 10 pieces |
| 10/20 | 10-20 pieces |
| 20/30 | 20-30 pieces |
| 30/40 | 30-40 pieces |
| 80/100 | 80-100 pieces |
| 100/120 | 100-120 pieces |
| 120/150 | 120-150 pieces |

#### Appearance Defects (Document Any)

- [ ] Viscera (gut material)
- [ ] Meat discoloration (purple/gray)
- [ ] Sand, grit, or shell fragments
- [ ] Pumpkin-colored meat
- [ ] IQF clumping
- [ ] Breaded clumping
- [ ] Yellowing from aging
- [ ] Dehydration spots

#### Quality Indicators

**Moisture Content**
- Dry Scallops: 74-82% moisture
  - Can be soaked up to 82% moisture
- Wet Scallops: 83-86% moisture
  - Indicates sodium tripolyphosphate (STP) use for moisture retention

**Organoleptic**

- [ ] **Odor**: Freshly caught, processed scallops
  - NO: Sour or ammonia
  - NO: Old freezer odors
  - NO: Chemical smells

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist
  - NO: Soft or mushy
  - NO: Rubbery (phosphate abuse)

- [ ] **Color**: Natural scallop color
  - NO: Purple/gray discoloration
  - NO: Translucency (phosphate indicator)

## SQUID/CALAMARI

### Inspection Methodology for Squid (Tubes, Rings, Tentacles)

#### Weight Assessment

- [ ] Record gross weight (packaging + glaze + squid)
- [ ] Select 1-2 lbs minimum subsample
- [ ] Record glaze-on weight

**De-glaze**
- [ ] Place in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30° angle
- [ ] Record glaze-off weight

#### Tube/Tentacle Specifications

- [ ] Measure tubes with ruler
- [ ] Record length in inches
- [ ] Calculate ratio of tubes to tentacles:
  ```
  Ratio Tubes = # Tubes / (# Tubes + # Tentacles)
  Ratio Tentacles = # Tentacles / (# Tubes + # Tentacles)
  ```
- [ ] Should have approximately equal numbers

#### Appearance Defects

- [ ] Pens and quills (hard structures)
- [ ] Beaks (mouth parts)
- [ ] IQF clumping
- [ ] Breaded clumping
- [ ] Yellowing from aging
- [ ] Dehydration spots

#### Organoleptic Assessment

- [ ] **Odor**: Freshly caught squid
  - NO: Sour or ammonia
  - NO: Old freezer odors
  - NO: Chemical smells

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist
  - NO: Soft or mushy

- [ ] **Color**: Natural squid color
  - NO: Discoloration (spoilage indicator)

## FIN FISH (FILLETS, STEAKS)

#### Sampling & Physical Inspection

- [ ] Pull samples from random locations
- [ ] Check for temperature abuse
- [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging and glaze)

#### Weight & Glaze Assessment

- [ ] Randomly select 6 fillets (~1-2 lbs)
- [ ] Record glaze-on weight

**De-glaze**
- [ ] Place in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30°
- [ ] Record glaze-off weight

**Calculations**
```
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Portion Size: Weigh 6 fillets, record in ounces
```

#### Portion Sizing

Standard portion weights (in ounces):
- 4 oz = 3-5 oz range
- 6 oz = 5-7 oz range
- 8 oz = 7-9 oz range
- 10 oz = 9-11 oz range

#### Appearance Defects (Document)

- [ ] Meat discoloration (green bellies)

- [ ] Bruises or handling marks
- [ ] Parasites or worms
- [ ] Poor workmanship (uneven trims)
- [ ] IQF clumping/married fillets
- [ ] Breaded clumping
- [ ] Yellowing from aging
- [ ] Dehydration spots

#### Organoleptic Assessment

- [ ] **Odor**: Freshly caught and processed fish
  - NO: Sour or ammonia
  - NO: Geosmin (muddy/grassy)
  - NO: Old freezer odors
  - NO: Chemical smells

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist

## FROG LEGS

### Inspection Methodology for Frog Legs

#### Physical Inspection

- [ ] Record gross weight (with packaging)
- [ ] Count frog legs while examining for defects
- [ ] Multiply count × avg poly wrap weight = tare
- [ ] Remove poly wrap from sampled frogs
- [ ] Record net weight

#### Glaze Assessment

- [ ] Place frog legs in No. 8 sieve
- [ ] Drain 2 minutes at 30° angle
- [ ] Record de-glazed weight

**Calculation**
```
% Glaze = ((Glaze On Wt - Glaze Off Wt) × 100) / Glaze On Wt
Count per Pound = (# Whole Frogs × 1) / Deglazed Net Wt
```

#### Sizing Standards

| Size | Count per lb |
|------|-------------|
| 2-4 | 2-4 legs per pound |
| 4-6 | 4-6 legs per pound |
| 6-8 | 6-8 legs per pound |
| 8-10 | 8-10 legs per pound |
| 10-12 | 10-12 legs per pound |
| 12-14 | 12-14 legs per pound |

#### Appearance Defects

- [ ] Excessive skin (not removed)
- [ ] Meat discoloration
- [ ] Excessive membrane attached
- [ ] Blemishes or marks
- [ ] Parasites present
- [ ] Poor workmanship (uneven trims)
- [ ] Toes improperly trimmed
- [ ] Saddle removed (if required)
- [ ] Yellowing from aging
- [ ] Dehydration spots

#### Organoleptic Assessment

- [ ] **Odor**: Freshly harvested frog
  - NO: Objectionable odors
  - NO: Sour or ammonia
  - NO: Muddy/grassy (geosmin)
  - NO: Old freezer odors
  - NO: Chemical smells

- [ ] **Texture**: Firm and moist
  - NO: Soft or mushy

- [ ] **Color**: Natural frog color
  - NO: Discoloration (spoilage)

## MOLLUSCAN SHELLFISH (OYSTERS, CLAMS, MUSSELS)

### Critical Requirements

- [ ] **MUST be from licensed processor** on Interstate Certified Shellfish Shippers List (ICSSL)
- [ ] **Verify dealer certification number**
- [ ] **Record harvest area labeling** (required)
- [ ] **Record harvest date** (required for traceability)
- [ ] **Temperature maintained**: ≤ 10°F
- [ ] **Time/Temperature Indicator** present and checked

### Receiving Documentation

- [ ] Container/trailer temperature
- [ ] Multiple internal product temperatures
- [ ] Time/temperature recorder (TTI/TTR) status
- [ ] Dealer cert # and harvest ID
- [ ] Harvest area code (e.g., "LA A-1 LA wild reef")
- [ ] Date received
- [ ] Inspector name and date

### Traceability Records Maintained

- [ ] Copies of tags on file
- [ ] Receiving logs with temperatures
- [ ] QC inspection results
- [ ] Labels and lot numbers
- [ ] Purchase orders and sales records
- [ ] Complete chain of custody documentation
